# Angular Momentum Non-Conservation during Ballistic Mass Transfer in Binary Star Systems

We challenge the assumption of angular momentum conservation in classical theories within binary star systems undergoing mass transfer. In such theories, it is predicted that the orbital separation expands when $q < 1$, and contracts when $q > 1$, where $q = M_{\mathrm{donor}}/M_{\mathrm{accretor}}$ is the mass ratio. However, these predictions rely on angular momentum conservation, which is not guaranteed during mass transfer. We therefore examine a ballistic approach to the transfer, in which transferred mass $\delta m$ carries the donor's instantaneous velocity and we analytically demonstrate angular momentum non-conservation. Specifically, $\Delta L < 0$ for every transfer increment, regardless of mass ratio, with cumulative loss $\Delta L / L \approx -f$, where $f$ is the fractional mass transferred and $L$ is the system's orbital angular momentum about the center of mass. Numerical simulations confirm this result by demonstrating orbital contraction and approximately 15\% angular momentum loss for all test cases. These results match our analytical derivations.
